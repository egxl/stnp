## Category 01 — Dispute Resolution

### Category Intro Copy
STNP's dispute resolution practice is the backbone of the firm. Our partners have represented major Indonesian and international clients at every level of the Indonesian court system — from initial filing at the District Court (*Pengadilan Negeri*) through to final appeal at the Supreme Court (*Mahkamah Agung*). Our lawyers are all registered members of PERADI, the Indonesian Bar Association, and are authorised to appear before any Indonesian court.

---

### Service 01.A — Commercial Litigation

**Tagline:** *Court-ready. Indonesia-wide.*

**Body:**
Commercial disputes in Indonesia demand more than legal knowledge — they require procedural command, local relationships, and the stamina to pursue a matter from first instance through appeal. STNP's litigation team has built that capability over years of representing major clients in civil and commercial cases: property companies, logistics firms, music labels, and financial institutions.

We are equipped to handle administrative litigation alongside civil commercial matters, giving clients a single, consistent team across all dispute channels. Every advocate on our team is registered with PERADI and may appear before any Indonesian court.

**Proof Points to surface:**
- Represented a publicly-listed property company (Tbk) from District Court to Supreme Court, including consumer PPJB disputes
- Represented PT Warner Music Indonesia in a Tort (PMH) lawsuit
- Represented a logistics company in unpaid invoice collections

---

### Service 01.B — Banking & Finance

**Tagline:** *From syndication to execution.*

**Body:**
STNP has advised both financial institutions and corporate borrowers on complex financing transactions — from syndicated credit facilities to the execution of security assets in debt agreements. Our work extends to Cessie (receivables transfer) transactions and regulatory compliance with OJK requirements.

We understand that in banking disputes, speed and credibility of counsel matter. Our team has handled bad-debt resolution, Cessie proceedings, and security execution for foreign banks operating in Indonesia.

**Proof Points to surface:**
- Represented a coal company in a **USD 120 million syndicated credit process**
- Represented a foreign bank in bad credit, Cessie, and security execution matters
- Assisted a technology lending company in Cessie agreement drafting

---

### Service 01.C — Bankruptcy & Insolvency

**Tagline:** *Curators, receivers, and restructuring counsel.*

**Body:**
Indonesia's bankruptcy and insolvency framework — governed by the Commercial Court (*Pengadilan Niaga*) — requires practitioners with deep procedural knowledge and, in many cases, official appointments. STNP's Daniel Nababan is a registered member of AKPI (Asosiasi Kurator dan Pengurus Indonesia), qualifying our team to act as court-appointed Curators and Receivers in bankruptcy proceedings.

We represent both creditors and debtors: filing PKPU (Suspension of Debt Payment Obligations) applications, managing boedel (bankruptcy estates), and executing liquidations. Our clients include Indonesian and international businesses facing complex insolvency situations.

**Proof Points to surface:**
- Acted as Curator in bankruptcy proceedings at the Commercial Court (Surabaya), managing assets in Kutai (East Kalimantan) and West Kalimantan
- Acted as Liquidator to liquidate several companies
- Handled consumer PKPU filings against Koperasi Pracico

---

### Service 01.D — Industrial Relations (Labor Law)

**Tagline:** *From company policy to dispute resolution.*

**Body:**
Indonesian labor law is complex and carries significant exposure for employers who get it wrong. STNP advises companies on the full employment lifecycle: drafting Company Regulations (*Peraturan Perusahaan*) and Collective Labor Agreements (*Perjanjian Kerja Bersama*), structuring Fixed-Term (*PKWT*) and Indefinite-Term (*PKWTT*) employment contracts, managing terminations and severance calculations, and representing clients before the Industrial Relations Court.

We work with both multinational corporations and domestic employers navigating Indonesia's Manpower Law (*Undang-Undang Ketenagakerjaan*) and the Omnibus Law amendments.

## Category 02 — Corporate & Commercial

### Category Intro Copy
STNP's corporate practice covers the full business lifecycle in Indonesia — from incorporation and licensing through to acquisitions, restructuring, and capital markets. Our partners have direct experience in high-value transactions, including pre-IPO preparation, M&A advisory, and cross-border investment structuring for both domestic and foreign investors.

---

### Service 02.A — General Corporate

**Tagline:** *Your legal partner from day one.*

**Body:**
Whether you are establishing a new entity, restructuring an existing business, or managing day-to-day legal exposure, STNP provides the full-spectrum corporate support that serious businesses require in Indonesia. We advise on company formation (PT PMA and PMDN), licensing, compliance, and corporate governance — and we remain available as ongoing legal counsel for operational matters as they arise.

Our experience spans sectors from property and plantation to technology and logistics, giving us the contextual depth to advise across industry lines.

---

### Service 02.B — Mergers & Acquisitions

**Tagline:** *Transaction counsel with teeth.*

**Body:**
M&A in Indonesia comes with layers of regulatory complexity: KPPU notification obligations, OJK approvals for listed entities, land rights due diligence, and sector-specific licensing transfers. STNP has guided both buyers and sellers through these layers — conducting legal due diligence, structuring share acquisitions, and managing post-transaction regulatory compliance.

We have advised on acquisitions in the plantation, mining, parking management, and forest licensing sectors. Our partners are familiar with both the commercial and procedural dimensions of Indonesian M&A, including KPPU filing requirements and PMA/PMDN structuring.

**Proof Points to surface:**
- Assisted the seller in share acquisition of a security parking company
- Assisted a foreign investment company in land acquisition
- Managed KPPU filing for company acquisition reporting
- Assisted in transfer of IUPHHK-HA (Forest Timber Production Licence)

---

### Service 02.C — Project Financing

**Tagline:** *Structured for scale.*

**Body:**
Large infrastructure, industrial, and plantation projects in Indonesia require legal frameworks as complex as the projects themselves. STNP has been involved in project financing transactions across multiple sectors — advising on security structures, syndicated facilities, and the regulatory approvals required to bring major projects to financial close.

Our team understands the interplay between Indonesian mining law, plantation regulations, and banking requirements — and can provide integrated advice that a generalist firm cannot.

**Proof Points to surface:**
- Syndicated credit process for a coal company: **USD 120 million**
- Gas price negotiation and sales/purchase, including supply to PT PLN (Persero)
- Infrastructure and plantation project financing across Indonesia

---

### Service 02.D — Intellectual Property

**Tagline:** *Protecting creative and commercial assets.*

**Body:**
Indonesia's IP framework — administered by DJKI (Direktorat Jenderal Kekayaan Intelektual) — covers patents, trademarks, copyrights, and trade secrets. STNP advises clients on IP registration, licensing, and enforcement, with particular depth in the entertainment and media sectors.

Our team has drafted and negotiated complex IP agreements in the film and music industries, including crew agreements, author agreements, cast agreements, economic rights transfer agreements, and circulation licenses.

**Proof Points to surface:**
- Provided IP counsel to PT Kharisma Starvision Plus (film production)
- Represented Sony Music Indonesia in legal matters
- Drafted economic rights transfer agreements and circulation licenses

## Category 03 — Industry Focus

### Category Intro Copy
Beyond our core practice groups, STNP has developed genuine sector-specific expertise in the industries that drive Indonesia's economy. Our work in energy, plantation, infrastructure, and tourism is not peripheral — it is at the core of how our partners built their careers. Clients in these sectors benefit from partners who understand both the legal framework and the commercial realities of their industry.

---

### Service 03.A — Energy, Mining & Forestry

**Tagline:** *Natural resources. High stakes.*

**Body:**
Indonesia's extractive industries operate under a dense regulatory framework — mining licenses (*IUP*), forestry permits (*IUPHHK*), environmental approvals, and energy sector compliance with ESDM and BPH Migas regulations. STNP's partners have advised both corporate clients and state-owned enterprises in this space, including PT PLN (Persero) and major coal companies.

We advise on license acquisition and transfers, sale-and-purchase of forest plantation production permits (*IUPHHK-HT*), and the transactional structures used in upstream energy deals.

**Proof Points to surface:**
- Represented a coal company for pre-IPO due diligence, acquisition, and USD 120M syndicated credit
- Gas sales and price negotiations with PT PLN (Persero)
- IUPHHK-HA transfer transactions in the natural forest sector

---

### Service 03.B — Infrastructure & Property

**Tagline:** *From permits to possession.*

**Body:**
Real property in Indonesia carries unique complexity: land rights (*HGB, HM, HP*), PPJB disputes, title due diligence, and the interaction between regional government permits and national land law. STNP has represented both developers and buyers — advising on land acquisitions by PMA companies, managing property litigation from District Court to Supreme Court, and assisting in permit and license structuring for infrastructure projects.

Our experience spans residential developers, commercial property companies, and infrastructure investors — including publicly listed companies (*Tbk*) with consumer-facing legal exposure.

**Proof Points to surface:**
- Represented PT Indonesian Paradise Property Tbk and PT Sentul City Tbk
- Land acquisition advisory for foreign investment companies (PMA)
- Represented a major property company in consumer PPJB disputes
- Represented PT Timor Properti Investindo

---

### Service 03.C — Plantation & Agriculture

**Tagline:** *Rooted in Indonesia's agricultural sector.*

**Body:**
Palm oil plantation law in Indonesia intersects land rights, environmental licensing, plasma plantation obligations, and spatial planning regulation. STNP has built a track record advising on plantation company acquisitions, land rights disputes, licensing issues (including HGU *Hak Guna Usaha*), and plasma plantation structures.

We have represented clients in disputes over plantation land areas and assisted in the transfer of operational permits — work that demands both transactional capability and willingness to litigate when negotiations fail.

**Proof Points to surface:**
- Palm oil plantation company acquisitions and licensing
- Plantation land area dispute resolution
- Plasma plantation fund disbursement agreements
- Represented PT Advanced Agri Indonesia

---

### Service 03.D — Tourism & Hospitality

**Tagline:** *Where investment meets regulation.*

**Body:**
Indonesia's tourism sector — from Bali villa portfolios to resort management companies — requires careful legal structuring for foreign investors and property owners. STNP advises on management agreements, lease agreements, and operating license structures for tourism-business entities, helping clients navigate the interface between property rights, regional permits, and hospitality sector regulation.

**Proof Points to surface:**
- Advised PT Manado Maju Wisata on tourism legal matters
- Lease and management agreement drafting for tourism-business entities
